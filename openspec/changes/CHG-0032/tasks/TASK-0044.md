## Risk

- **Wave-5 supplement attribution is best-effort.** The 65 architecture-defect findings were clustered into ~25 new themes + ~30 reinforcements based on inspection of the per-stream findings in this session's context. A re-clusterer working from `findings/GOV-findings.md` + `findings/SEC-findings.md` + `findings/GOVDEV-findings.md` may produce different theme boundaries. The supplement's compact-list format (theme ID + 1-line summary + source citation) is designed to make re-clustering tractable — themes can be re-named, merged, or split without losing the source-finding provenance.
- **ACGR ~91% destabilises the convergence projection.** The qd-triage.md §7 prediction model is now empirically falsified for Wave 5. Wave 6's ACGR is uncertain; it could be high (if structural-governance is theme-dense like attitudinal-governance was) or moderate (if compression-lens-security is sparse as expected). The methodology codification ADR cannot yet pin a convergence threshold with confidence.
- **The QD admission rule is vindicated; the convergence threshold is questioned.** Wave-5 streams hit Tier A 4-for-4 — the rule "admit on cell-fill" worked. But the threshold "ACGR < 5% for two consecutive waves" assumed cells are sparse-by-default; empirically they are not. The methodology ADR has a choice: lower the threshold, change the denominator definition, or accept higher residual κ as the audit's terminal state.
