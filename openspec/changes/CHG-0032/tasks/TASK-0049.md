## Headline findings

- **867 sub-agent findings classified.** Within-batch SIGNAL rate: 99.3% (subagents defaulted to SIGNAL per instructions). Within-batch non-SIGNAL: 6 (3 METHOD_ARTIFACT or equivalent, 3 within-batch duplicates).
- **Cross-batch redundancy is substantial: ~210 findings (24%) flagged as likely duplicates of findings in other batches.** Per-stream true-σ after cross-batch dedup: median ~73%, range 25% (SECDISTILL — largely re-discovered SEC's findings via compression) to 100% (RED2, META, STRUCTGOV — covered cells no prior stream touched).
- **42 dense duplicate-claim clusters identified**, accounting for ~700+ findings. The corpus's effective unique-defect surface is ~120-150 (42 clusters + ~80-110 long-tail singletons), ~6-7× smaller than the raw finding count.
- **1 hard contradiction**: PREM2-PROC-009 vs META-CRIT-003 (both about ADR-0003 existence; PREM2 is retracted, as is FIRST-CRIT-01 with the same defect class).
- **1 framing-difference quasi-contradiction**: PM-CRIT-04 vs DEVIL-PRO-16 (productivity vs workflow framing of phase-exit ordering; both findings stand).

## Implications surfaced (for methodology ADR drafting)

1. **σ should be measured at the unique-claim-cluster level**, not the raw-finding level. The 99.3% within-batch SIGNAL is misleading; per-stream true σ (median ~73%) is the honest measurement.
2. **ACGR convergence regression is partly illusory.** Waves 5-7's high raw ACGR (~91%/~105%/~87%) over-counts framing-refinements of existing clusters as new themes. True unique-claim ACGR for those waves is probably 30-50%, not 87-105%. The audit may be substantially closer to convergence than headline ACGR suggested.
3. **42 clusters concentrate in 5 macro-areas** (path/wrapper/substrate drift; governance machinery; security primitives; bootstrap/scale; test/CI discipline). These are the natural resolution-CHG groupings.
4. **`|meaningful_cells|` denominator should be re-pinned at 25-35**, not ~55. Conservative cell-pruning would drop ~15-20 empty/E-class cells; moderate pruning would likely collapse Axis D into Axis C (severity inherited from method, not discovered from theme).
5. **QD admission rule (cell-fill) is vindicated.** Cell-targeted Wave-5/6/7 streams all hit Tier A on the rule's criterion. The rule works; the threshold and denominator need empirical re-calibration.

## Notes

- **Subagent prompt design.** Each subagent received the §2.2 signal-filter definition, the same 7-category classification schema, references to `consolidated.md` for theme context and `corrections.md` for pre-flagged classifications, and instructions to default to SIGNAL conservatively. Stream-batch assignment was by wave to minimise within-batch duplicate-rate (Wave-1 streams cross-confirm heavily; Wave-5/6/7 streams cross-confirm less because they target distinct cells).
- **Why default-to-SIGNAL produced 99% σ.** The subagents took the conservatism instruction literally; nearly every finding is concrete enough and actionable enough at face value to pass the filter. The real signal is cross-batch deduplication, which the CROSS-BATCH FLAGS sections captured well.
- **Verifiable spot-checks.** META-CRIT-001's claim that PROSE doesn't mention "the paper" was verified by grep (PROSE-findings.md contains zero references). META-CRIT-003's claim that ADR-0003 exists was verified by directory listing. The classifications of FIRST-CRIT-01, MARY-SERIOUS-12, and PREM2-PROC-009 as non-SIGNAL all derive from META verifications already in `corrections.md`.

## Risk

- **Cluster boundary judgement.** The 42 clusters are aggregated from subagent-flagged cross-batch duplicates. Different subagents flagged with different aggressiveness; some long-tail clusters may be over- or under-merged. The 5-macro-area groupings in §6 are coarser and more robust to boundary-judgement noise.
- **Within-batch SIGNAL over-classification.** Subagents likely under-detected METHOD_ARTIFACT and NOT_ACTIONABLE. A second triage pass with stricter signal-filter calibration would probably surface another 30-60 method-artifact-class findings. Not done in this TASK; deferred to methodology ADR's red-test phase.
- **The unique-claim surface size (~120-150) is itself an estimate.** Could be as low as ~80 (if long-tail singletons cluster more than enumerated) or as high as ~200 (if some 42-clusters subdivide on deeper inspection). The 5 macro-areas are stable regardless of the exact micro-count.
- **The convergence-threshold re-interpretation in §6.4 is preliminary.** Saying Waves 5-7 produced 30-50% true unique-claim ACGR (vs headline 87-105%) is an estimate from cluster-membership patterns, not a measurement. The methodology ADR's audit-meta-test suite should mechanise true ACGR before any "we're converged" claim is made.
