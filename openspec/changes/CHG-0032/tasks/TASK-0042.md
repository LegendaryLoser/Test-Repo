# TASK-0042 — Architecture audit Wave 5 (4 streams targeting Tier-1 empty cells)

## Intent

Per [`qd-triage.md`](../../../_bmad-output/knowledge/audit/2026-05-17-architecture/qd-triage.md) §8, run Wave 5: 4 streams targeting the 4 high-importance empty cells (3 Tier-1 governance/security cells + 1 meta-review). Wave 5 is the first audit wave executed under the QD admission rule: streams are admitted iff they fill an empty cell on the four-axis behavioural map and clear the σ ≥ 0.30 competence floor. Convergence is measured by ACGR re-computation rather than raw marginal novelty (replaces COMPOSITE-V2 Gate 6).

The 4 cells, their methods, and the raw counts produced:

| Prefix | Cell (Lens × Temporal × Decomposition × Severity) | Method | Invocation | Findings (CRIT/SER/PROC) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `GOV-` | attitudinal × current × focused × governance | `validate-prd` with governance-focused biasing | general-purpose subagent invoking `bmad-validate-prd` | 22 (5 / 14 / 3) |
| `SEC-` | persona × current × holistic × security/ops | security-engineer persona, holistic current-state | general-purpose subagent with custom persona prompt | 21 (7 / 9 / 5) |
| `GOVDEV-` | contrarian × current × focused × governance | devil's-advocate with governance focus | general-purpose subagent invoking `bmad-advanced-elicitation` (devil's-advocate technique) | 22 (5 / 11 / 6) |
| `META-` | meta × * × * × * (candidate 5th axis) | reasoning-tree meta-auditor over the existing 35-stream corpus | general-purpose subagent with custom meta-auditor prompt; reads `findings/`, `consolidated.md`, `qd-triage.md` | 19 (4 / 12 / 3) |
| **Wave 5 total** | | | | **84 (21 / 46 / 17)** |

Invocation rationale derived from Wave-4 empirical evidence: STAKE and COUNTER (the two highest-κ streams in the entire corpus, 10 and 9 sole themes respectively) were spawned as general-purpose subagents with custom persona/method prompts, not via named BMAD skills. This established the precedent for cell-targeted methods whose target cell has no pre-existing BMAD skill. `GOV-` and `GOVDEV-` retain BMAD-skill invocation (bmad-validate-prd and bmad-advanced-elicitation respectively) because the cell is reachable with a focused biasing of an existing skill. `SEC-` and `META-` use general-purpose because no BMAD skill maps to their cells.

## Notes

- **Method-vs-model diversity finding holds.** Wave-5 deliberately targeted new methods (not model permutations of existing ones). This is consistent with the TASK-0037 empirical finding that methodology variance dominates marginal novelty. Wave-5 streams are all opus; sonnet permutations of Wave-5 methods are a separate question deferred to a possible Wave 6.
- **`META-` is the first meta-axis stream.** Its findings audit the audit, not the architecture. Per `qd-triage.md` §8, treating "meta" as a candidate 5th axis is itself an extension of the QD framework — a decision for the methodology codification ADR (queued).
- **`GOV-` and `GOVDEV-` are deliberately same-cell-paired.** They share the governance focus but differ on lens (attitudinal vs. contrarian) and decomposition technique. Per QD admission, both can co-occupy the cell pair if they sit on the σ × κ Pareto frontier — first ACGR after Wave-5 consolidation will determine whether both stay admitted or one dominates the other.
- **No new REQ.** TASK-0042 covers REQ-AUDIT-0001 (corpus-persistence obligation) by extending the corpus and the extraction tool. No new requirement is authored.

## Risk

- **ACGR not yet re-measured.** Wave-5 was admitted on cell-targeting (per `qd-triage.md` §8 admission rule); the actual ACGR contribution is unknown until thematic consolidation runs. If the 4 Wave-5 streams turn out to reinforce existing themes rather than open new (cell, theme) pairs, the convergence trajectory is faster than projected. Conversely if they open many new themes, Wave 6 becomes more important.
- **`META-` surfaced 4 CRIT-class retractions and 12 SER-class fragilities** against the existing corpus. Specifically: THEME-P mis-attributed to PROSE (actual surfacers are IND/ADVS/STRUCT/INHER/WIN/SALLY); `findings-index.md` structurally incomplete (documents only Waves 1-3, missing Wave 4 entirely); `FIRST-CRIT-01` is a self-inflicted method artifact (FIRST misread its own prompt and declared ADR-0003 out of scope when it was in scope); STAKE/COUNTER raw counts in `qd-triage.md` inflated. Plus σ × κ scoring errors, theme over-generalisations, and the corpus reproducing its own diagnosed failure mode (anonymous citation hazard) when invoking arXiv 2602.09341. Acting on these retractions (annotating or removing the cited findings in `consolidated.md`, fixing the qd-triage attributions, completing `findings-index.md`) is the FIRST action of the next-session consolidation task — these corrections must land before any joint architecture+implementation consolidation, otherwise the corrections compound. The full retraction list is preserved in `findings/META-findings.md`.
- **General-purpose subagent invocation is a departure for `SEC-` and `META-`.** Two of the four streams do not invoke a named BMAD skill. This is consistent with Wave-4 precedent (STAKE, COUNTER) but is the first time the audit catalog admits cell-targeted methods outside the BMAD skill registry as a deliberate policy. The methodology codification ADR will need to address whether the catalog has two classes (BMAD-skill methods + general-purpose-prompted methods) or one class.
